HOW PERSONAL EMAIL DETECTION WORKS
=====================================
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Relevant file: Backend/email_advising/personal_guardrails.py


=====================================
WHAT IT DOES
=====================================

Before any confidence scoring or template matching happens, every incoming
email is scanned for personal or sensitive content. If flagged, the email:

  1. Gets status = "personal" (never "auto" or "review")
  2. NEVER gets auto-sent, no matter how high the confidence score
  3. Gets a special draft reply with mental health resources
  4. Is shown in the UI under "Needs Review" with a personal flag

This is a hard override — no confidence threshold can bypass it.


=====================================
WHAT TRIGGERS THE FLAG
=====================================

The detector checks for two types of matches:

--- Keywords (word-boundary matched) ---

Mental health:
  anxiety, depression, depressed, stressed, overwhelmed, therapy,
  counseling, counselor, suicidal, suicide, mental health, panic attack,
  eating disorder

Medical / health:
  hospitalized, hospitalization, medical leave, illness, disability,
  accommodation

Financial hardship:
  financial aid, hardship, can't afford, tuition help, financial hardship

Family / personal emergencies:
  family emergency, bereavement, death in family, grief, domestic violence,
  restraining order, homelessness, homeless, abuse

Discrimination / safety:
  harassment, discrimination, title ix, assault, crisis

Academic distress (personal framing):
  dropping out, failing, academic probation, suspension

--- Phrases (substring matched) ---

  "i don't know what to do"
  "i need help urgently"
  "i'm struggling" / "i am struggling"
  "i can't cope" / "i cant cope"
  "please help me"
  "i feel hopeless"
  "i'm in crisis" / "i am in crisis"
  "i have nowhere to turn"
  "i feel like giving up"
  "i can't take it anymore" / "i cant take it anymore"
  "i need someone to talk to"
  "i'm not okay" / "i am not okay"
  "i don't feel safe" / "i dont feel safe"


=====================================
HOW MATCHING WORKS
=====================================

1. The email body is lowercased.
2. Keywords use word-boundary regex (\b) so partial matches are avoided.
   Example: "class" does NOT match "classification".
3. Phrases are matched as literal substrings (case-insensitive).
4. If ANY keyword or phrase matches → is_personal = True.
5. All matched keywords and phrases are recorded for the advisor to see.

This runs BEFORE confidence scoring and BEFORE template selection.
Order of operations in api.py > ingest_email():
  1. Run advisor.process_query() to get confidence + suggested reply
  2. Run personal_detector.check() on the email body
  3. If personal → override status and suggested reply
  4. Store in database


=====================================
WHAT THE ADVISOR SEES
=====================================

When an email is flagged as personal, the suggested reply is replaced
with a standard message:

  "Hello [name],

   Thank you for reaching out. Your message has been flagged for personal
   attention from our advising team. An advisor will follow up with you
   directly.

   If you need immediate support, please contact:
   • Columbia Counseling and Psychological Services (CPS): (212) 854-2878
   • Columbia Health: (212) 854-2284

   Best,
   Academic Advising Team"

The advisor should ALWAYS review this before sending and personalize
the response based on what the student actually wrote.


=====================================
HOW TO CUSTOMIZE THE DETECTION
=====================================

The keyword and phrase lists are defined as constants in personal_guardrails.py:
  DEFAULT_KEYWORDS  - list of individual words/terms
  DEFAULT_PHRASES   - list of multi-word phrases

To add a new keyword: add it to DEFAULT_KEYWORDS.
To add a new phrase: add it to DEFAULT_PHRASES.
Restart the backend after any changes.

The PersonalEmailDetector class can also be instantiated with custom lists
if needed for testing or specialized use cases.


=====================================
IMPORTANT NOTES
=====================================

- False positives are acceptable and expected. It's better to route an
  email to human review unnecessarily than to auto-reply to a student
  who is in distress.

- The word "financial aid" is on the list — this means a routine question
  like "Where do I find financial aid information?" WILL be flagged as personal.
  This is intentional. If you want to reduce false positives for financial aid
  questions, remove "financial aid" from the keywords and rely on the more
  specific phrases like "financial hardship" instead.

- "failing" and "dropping out" are on the list. A student asking "What
  happens if I fail a class?" will be flagged. Again, this is conservative
  by design — when in doubt, a human reviews it.

- There is no "confidence" score for personal detection. It's binary:
  any match → flagged.
