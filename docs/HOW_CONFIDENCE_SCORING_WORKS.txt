HOW CONFIDENCE SCORING WORKS
=============================
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Relevant files: Backend/email_advising/advisor.py
                Backend/email_advising/similarity.py
                Backend/email_advising/text_processing.py
                Backend/email_advising/models.py


=====================================
THE BIG PICTURE
=====================================

When a student email comes in, the system needs to decide:
  1. Do I know how to answer this?      → confidence score
  2. Am I confident enough to auto-send? → compare to auto_send_threshold
  3. Should it go to a human advisor?    → compare to review_threshold

The confidence score is a number from 0.0 to 1.0. Higher = more certain.

Default thresholds (set in models.py > ConfidenceSettings):
  auto_send_threshold  = 0.95   (send automatically without human review)
  review_threshold     = 0.55   (below this, always route to a human)
  ambiguity_gap        = 0.08   (if top 2 matches are within 0.08 of each other, route to human)


=====================================
STEP 1: TEXT PREPROCESSING
=====================================

File: text_processing.py

Before any matching happens, the email text is cleaned up:

  1. Lowercase + strip accents (é → e, etc.)
  2. Remove punctuation, collapse whitespace
  3. Tokenize into individual words
  4. Remove stopwords (a, the, is, my, your, how, etc.)
  5. Augment tokens with:
     a. Domain synonyms — e.g., "drop" → also adds "withdraw", "withdrawal", "remove"
                                "enroll" → also adds "register", "registration"
     b. Bigrams — consecutive word pairs, e.g., ["drop", "course"] → "drop_course"

This means a student writing "I want to withdraw from my class" and one writing
"I need to drop a course" will produce very similar token sets after augmentation.


=====================================
STEP 2: SCORING EACH KNOWLEDGE BASE ARTICLE
=====================================

File: advisor.py > EmailAdvisor.rank_articles()

For EACH article in the knowledge base, the system computes 5 signals:

--- Signal 1: TF-IDF Score (weight: 0.2) ---

File: similarity.py > TfIdfVectorizer

TF-IDF stands for Term Frequency - Inverse Document Frequency. It measures how
semantically relevant the email is to a knowledge base article.

How it's built:
  - At startup, every article's utterances + categories + subject are tokenized
    and augmented, forming one "document" per article.
  - A vocabulary is built from all tokens across all documents.
  - Each word gets an IDF weight = log((1 + N) / (1 + df)) + 1.0
    where N = total articles, df = how many articles contain that word.
    Common words across many articles get low IDF weight.
    Rare, specific words (like "petitioning") get high IDF weight.

How it scores a query:
  - The email text is also tokenized and augmented.
  - A TF-IDF vector is computed for the query.
  - Cosine similarity is calculated between the query vector and each article vector.
  - The full email AND each individual sentence are scored; the max is taken.

Result: a float between 0.0 and 1.0.

--- Signal 2: Jaccard Utterance Similarity (weight: 0.5 — highest weight) ---

Each knowledge base article has a list of "utterances": example phrasings of
questions that belong to that article (e.g., "How do I drop a course?",
"Can I withdraw from a class?").

Jaccard similarity = |intersection| / |union| of two token sets.

Example:
  Query tokens:     {drop, course, semester}
  Utterance tokens: {drop, class, semester}
  Intersection: {drop, semester} → size 2
  Union: {drop, course, semester, class} → size 4
  Jaccard = 2/4 = 0.50

Both raw tokens AND augmented tokens are tried; the best score is kept.

The email is also split into individual sentences, and each sentence is
scored against each utterance. The max across all sentence/utterance pairs wins.

--- Signal 3: Coverage Signals (weight: 0.2) ---

Two coverage metrics measure partial overlap quality:

  query_coverage    = |intersection| / |query tokens|
                      (what fraction of the query's words match the utterance?)

  utterance_coverage = |intersection| / |utterance tokens|
                       (what fraction of the utterance's words appear in the query?)

coverage_signal = average of these two.

These catch cases where Jaccard is dragged down by a long union,
even though the meaningful words all matched.

--- Signal 4: Category Overlap (weight: 0.1) ---

Each article also belongs to categories (e.g., "registration", "financial aid").
The query tokens are compared to category tokens via Jaccard.

This is a weak supporting signal — it helps distinguish between articles
that have similar utterances but belong to different topics.

--- Signal 5: Article Overlap ---

Similar to utterance similarity but across the entire article's token set
(all utterances + categories + subject combined). Not directly weighted in
the formula but used as a fallback comparison.


=====================================
STEP 3: BLENDING THE SIGNALS INTO ONE CONFIDENCE SCORE
=====================================

File: advisor.py lines 216-235

Formula (non-exact match case):

  coverage_signal    = (query_coverage + utterance_coverage) / 2
  base_confidence    = (0.5 * jaccard_utterance)
                     + (0.2 * coverage_signal)
                     + (0.2 * tfidf_score)
                     + (0.1 * category_overlap)

--- Special cases ---

EXACT MATCH:
  If the query tokens exactly match any utterance's tokens → confidence = 1.0

WEAK SIGNAL PENALTY:
  If coverage_signal < 0.15 AND jaccard < 0.2:
    base_confidence *= 0.6
  (Very little overlap = we're probably not on topic — penalize heavily.)

FLOOR OVERRIDES (snap up to minimum confidence if similarity is strong):
  jaccard >= 0.85  → confidence at least 0.92
  jaccard >= 0.70  → confidence at least 0.85
  jaccard >= 0.55 AND coverage >= 0.35 → confidence at least 0.78
  coverage >= 0.55 AND category_overlap >= 0.1 AND jaccard >= 0.5 → at least 0.85

HARD BOUNDS:
  Final confidence is clamped to [0.05, 0.97].
  (Never 0 or 1 unless exact match.)


=====================================
STEP 4: ROUTING DECISION
=====================================

File: advisor.py > process_query()

After all articles are scored and sorted by confidence:

  1. Top match confidence < review_threshold (0.55)?
     → No suitable match found → route to human (needs_review)

  2. Second-best match score >= review_threshold AND gap between top two < ambiguity_gap (0.08)?
     → Two plausible answers, system is unsure which → route to human (needs_review)

  3. Top match confidence >= review_threshold but < auto_send_threshold (0.95)?
     → Good match, but not confident enough to send → draft created, goes to needs_review

  4. Top match confidence >= auto_send_threshold (0.95) AND no missing template placeholders?
     → AUTO SEND → response sent automatically

  5. Good confidence match but template has unfilled placeholders?
     → Route to human so the advisor can fill in the missing details


=====================================
QUICK REFERENCE: CONFIDENCE BANDS
=====================================

  0.05 – 0.54  → Needs review (low confidence, no match found)
  0.55 – 0.94  → Needs review (match found, but below auto-send bar)
  0.95 – 0.97  → Auto send (high confidence, complete template)
  1.00          → Auto send (exact token match to a known utterance)

Note: A score of 0.95+ but with missing template placeholders still goes to
needs_review, regardless of confidence.


=====================================
EXAMPLE WALKTHROUGH
=====================================

Student email: "Hi, when does registration close for next semester?"

After preprocessing:
  Tokens: {registration, close, next, semester}
  Augmented: {registration, close, next, semester, register, enroll,
              closes, deadline, end, registration_close, close_next, next_semester}

Suppose the KB has an article "Registration Deadlines" with utterances like:
  "When does course registration close?"
  "What is the registration deadline?"

Utterance tokens (example): {course, registration, close}
Augmented utterance tokens: {course, registration, close, class, register, enroll,
                              closes, deadline, end, course_registration, registration_close}

Jaccard (augmented):
  Query augmented:     {registration, close, ..., register, enroll, deadline, ...}
  Utterance augmented: {course, registration, close, ..., register, enroll, deadline, ...}
  Lots of overlap → high Jaccard, e.g., ~0.75

With jaccard=0.75, floor override kicks in → confidence >= 0.85
This clears review_threshold (0.55), but is still below auto_send_threshold (0.95).
→ Result: Needs review, but a draft reply is prepared using the template.

If the student wrote a more specific, identical phrasing to a known utterance,
Jaccard could hit 0.9+ → confidence 0.95+ → auto send.


=====================================
HOW TO ADJUST THRESHOLDS
=====================================

Thresholds are configurable via the Settings tab in the UI, or directly in:
  Backend/email_advising/models.py > ConfidenceSettings

  auto_send_threshold: lower this to auto-send more aggressively
                       (e.g., 0.85 = send anything that scores 85%+)
  review_threshold:    lower this to reduce "no match" escalations
  ambiguity_gap:       increase this to be more conservative when two
                       articles score similarly close

Caution: lowering auto_send_threshold too much means the system may send
incorrect or incomplete replies without advisor review.
